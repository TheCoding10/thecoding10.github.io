Unit 1: Valuation Fundamentals

Master the core principles and concepts underlying company valuation methods.

Intrinsic Value Concept

Understand intrinsic value as the theoretical fair value of a company based on fundamental analysis.

Value vs Price Distinction

Learn the critical difference between market price and intrinsic value in investment decision-making.

Time Value of Money

Master present value concepts and discounting techniques essential for all valuation methods.

Risk and Return Relationship

Understand how risk affects required returns and discount rates in valuation models.

Valuation Approaches Overview

Survey the three main valuation approaches: asset-based, market-based, and income-based methods.

Cost of Capital Basics

Learn the fundamental concepts of cost of capital and its role in valuation discount rates.

Terminal Value Concepts

Understand how to estimate the value of cash flows beyond the explicit forecast period.

Valuation Purpose and Context

Recognize how valuation purpose affects methodology selection and value interpretation.

Unit 2: Financial Statement Analysis

Analyze financial statements to understand company performance and prepare for valuation.

Income Statement Analysis

Dissect revenue, expenses, and profitability trends to understand operational performance.

Balance Sheet Evaluation

Assess asset quality, liability structure, and equity composition for financial health insights.

Cash Flow Statement Review

Analyze operating, investing, and financing cash flows to assess liquidity and cash generation.

Financial Ratio Analysis

Calculate and interpret liquidity, profitability, efficiency, and leverage ratios for comprehensive analysis.

Quality of Earnings Assessment

Evaluate the sustainability and cash backing of reported earnings through quality analysis.

Trend Analysis Techniques

Identify patterns and trends in financial performance over multiple periods.

Peer Comparison Methods

Compare company metrics against industry peers and benchmarks for relative assessment.

Red Flags Identification

Recognize warning signs of financial distress, accounting irregularities, or management issues.

Unit 3: Discounted Cash Flow (DCF) Modeling

Build comprehensive DCF models to estimate intrinsic company value.

Free Cash Flow Calculation

Master the calculation of free cash flow to the firm and free cash flow to equity.

Revenue Forecasting

Build detailed revenue projections using various forecasting techniques and market analysis.
