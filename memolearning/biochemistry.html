Unit 1: Introduction to Biochemistry

Explore the chemistry of life and biological molecules.

What is Biochemistry

Understand the molecular basis of biological processes.

Molecules Life Chemistry
Biochemistry studies the chemical processes and substances within living organisms, bridging biology and chemistry to understand life at the molecular level.
Key areas: protein structure, enzyme catalysis, metabolism, genetics

Chemical Basis of Life

Learn about the fundamental chemistry underlying biological systems.

Elements Bonds Molecules
Living systems are built from a limited set of elements, primarily carbon, hydrogen, oxygen, nitrogen, phosphorus, and sulfur.

Energy in Biological Systems

Understand how energy flows through living systems.

ATP Energy
Living systems are thermodynamically open systems that maintain order by consuming energy and producing entropy.

Biochemical Techniques

Explore methods used to study biological molecules.

Techniques Analysis
Common techniques: chromatography, electrophoresis, spectroscopy, X-ray crystallography

Unit 18: Advanced Topics and Applications

Protein Engineering

Learn about designing proteins with novel functions.

Design Engineering Function
Protein engineering uses computational design and directed evolution to create proteins with enhanced or novel properties for therapeutic and industrial applications.

Metabolic Engineering

Understand how to modify cellular metabolism for biotechnology.

Pathways Optimization
Metabolic engineering redirects cellular metabolism to produce valuable compounds like pharmaceuticals, biofuels, and chemicals.
