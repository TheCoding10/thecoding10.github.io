Unit 1: Corporate Finance Foundations

Understand the fundamental principles and objectives of corporate financial management.

Role of Corporate Finance

Learn the core functions of corporate finance including investment decisions, financing decisions, and dividend policy.

Financial Manager Responsibilities

Understand the duties and responsibilities of financial managers in maximizing firm value and managing resources.

Maximizing Shareholder Value

Explore the primary goal of financial management and how decisions should align with shareholder wealth maximization.

Agency Problems and Costs

Identify conflicts of interest between managers and shareholders and understand agency costs and mitigation strategies.

Financial Markets Overview

Learn about money markets, capital markets, and how corporations interact with financial markets for funding.

Corporate Governance Basics

Understand the framework of rules and practices that govern corporate behavior and accountability to stakeholders.

Financial Goal Setting

Learn how to establish clear financial objectives and metrics to guide corporate financial decision-making.

Stakeholder Relationships

Explore the balance between shareholder interests and other stakeholders including employees, customers, and creditors.

Unit 2: Financial Statements Analysis

Master reading and interpreting financial statements for business decision-making.

Income Statement Analysis

Learn to analyze revenue, expenses, and profitability trends using income statement data and ratios.

Balance Sheet Components

Understand assets, liabilities, and equity structure and how they reflect a company's financial position.

Cash Flow Statement

Analyze operating, investing, and financing cash flows to assess liquidity and cash generation capability.

Statement of Equity Changes

Track changes in shareholder equity including retained earnings, stock issuances, and dividend payments.

Financial Ratios Calculation

Master liquidity, profitability, efficiency, and leverage ratios for comprehensive financial analysis.

Trend Analysis Techniques

Use horizontal and vertical analysis to identify patterns and changes in financial performance over time.

Industry Benchmarking

Compare company performance against industry averages and peer companies for relative assessment.

Financial Statement Quality

Evaluate the reliability and quality of financial statements and identify potential red flags or manipulations.

Unit 3: Time Value of Money

Learn fundamental concepts of present value, future value, and discounting techniques.

Present Value Calculations

Master the technique of discounting future cash flows to determine their current worth using discount rates.

Future Value Concepts

Learn to calculate the future worth of current investments using compound interest and growth rates.

Annuity Valuations

Value ordinary annuities, annuities due, and growing annuities for financial planning and investment analysis.

Perpetuity Calculations

Understand how to value perpetual cash flows and growing perpetuities for long-term investment evaluation.

Compounding Periods

Learn the impact of different compounding frequencies on investment returns and loan calculations.
