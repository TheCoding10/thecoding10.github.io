Unit 18: Cosmology and Particle Physics

Connect particle physics with the evolution and structure of the universe.

Big Bang Nucleosynthesis

Understand how the first elements formed in the early universe.

Nucleosynthesis Big Bang
The observed abundances of light elements (H, He, Li) provide strong evidence for the Big Bang and constrain particle physics.

Dark Matter

Explore the mysterious matter that makes up 85% of all matter.

Dark Matter WIMPs
Dark matter requires physics beyond the Standard Model. Leading candidates include WIMPs, axions, and sterile neutrinos.

Cosmic Rays

Study high-energy particles from space and their interactions.

Cosmic Rays Ultra-High Energy
Cosmic rays reach energies up to 10²⁰ eV, far beyond any terrestrial accelerator, providing a natural laboratory for particle physics.

Inflation and Early Universe

Learn how particle physics explains the universe's rapid early expansion.

Inflation Scalar Fields
Inflation theory uses quantum field theory to explain the universe's flatness, homogeneity, and the origin of cosmic structure.
