Unit 1: Complex Numbers and the Complex Plane

Build the foundation with complex number arithmetic and geometric visualization.

Complex Number Arithmetic

Master operations with complex numbers z = a + bi. Learn addition, multiplication, division, and the properties that make ℂ a field extension of ℝ.

Rectangular and Polar Forms

Convert between z = a + bi and z = r(cos θ + i sin θ) = re^(iθ). Understand when each form is most useful for computations.

De Moivre's Theorem

Apply (cos θ + i sin θ)^n = cos(nθ) + i sin(nθ) to compute powers and roots. Understand the geometric interpretation of multiplication as rotation and scaling.

Roots of Complex Numbers

Find all nth roots of complex numbers. Understand how the n roots are evenly distributed around a circle in the complex plane.

Complex Plane Geometry

Visualize complex numbers as points in the Argand diagram. Learn how complex operations correspond to geometric transformations.

Stereographic Projection

Understand the mapping between the complex plane and the Riemann sphere. Learn how this provides a geometric model for the extended complex plane.

Extended Complex Plane

Study ℂ ∪ {∞} and understand the point at infinity. Learn how this compactification is essential for certain theorems and applications.

Geometric Interpretations

Understand |z₁ - z₂| as distance, arg(z₁/z₂) as angle between vectors, and how complex multiplication combines rotation and scaling.

Unit 2: Analytic Functions

Learn the fundamental concept that distinguishes complex analysis from real analysis.

Complex Derivatives

Define f'(z₀) = lim[z→z₀] (f(z) - f(z₀))/(z - z₀). Understand why this limit must be independent of the direction of approach in ℂ.

Cauchy-Riemann Equations

Learn the necessary conditions ∂u/∂x = ∂v/∂y and ∂u/∂y = -∂v/∂x for f = u + iv to be analytic. Understand their geometric meaning.

Analytic vs Holomorphic

Understand that complex differentiability at a point vs. in a neighborhood leads to the equivalence of "analytic" and "holomorphic" functions.

Harmonic Functions

Study functions satisfying Laplace's equation ∇²u = 0. Learn the connection between harmonic functions and real/imaginary parts of analytic functions.

Harmonic Conjugates

For harmonic function u, find harmonic conjugate v such that f = u + iv is analytic. Understand uniqueness up to constants.

Conformal Mappings

Learn that analytic functions with non-zero derivative preserve angles. Understand the geometric significance of conformality.

Examples of Analytic Functions

Study polynomials, exponential, and other elementary functions. Learn to verify analyticity using Cauchy-Riemann equations.

Singularities

Identify points where functions fail to be analytic. Learn about removable singularities, poles, essential singularities, and branch points.

Unit 3: Elementary Functions

Extend familiar real functions to the complex domain and understand their new properties.

Complex Exponential

Define e^z = e^x(cos y + i sin y) for z = x + iy. Learn Euler's formula e^(iθ) = cos θ + i sin θ and the periodicity e^(z+2πi) = e^z.

Complex Logarithm

Define log z as the inverse of e^z. Understand multivaluedness and the need for branch cuts. Learn about principal branches and logarithmic branches.

Complex Trigonometric Functions

Define sin z = (e^(iz) - e^(-iz))/(2i) and cos z = (e^(iz) + e^(-iz))/2. Learn how these extend real trigonometric functions and their new properties.

Complex Hyperbolic Functions

Study sinh z, cosh z, and tanh z in the complex plane. Understand their relationship to trigonometric functions and periodicity properties.

Complex Powers

Define z^w = e^(w log z) for complex z and w. Understand multivaluedness and how to choose appropriate branches for applications.

Inverse Functions

Study inverse trigonometric and hyperbolic functions in ℂ. Learn about branch cuts and principal values for multivalued inverse functions.

Branch Cuts and Principal Values

Understand how to make multivalued functions single-valued by introducing branch cuts. Learn standard conventions for principal branches.

Riemann Surfaces

Visualize multivalued functions on Riemann surfaces where each "sheet" corresponds to a different branch. Understand how this resolves multivaluedness.

Unit 4: Complex Integration

Master contour integration and prove the fundamental theorems of complex analysis.

Complex Line Integrals

Define ∫_C f(z) dz for complex functions along curves C. Learn how to parameterize curves and compute integrals directly.

Contours and Paths

Study piecewise smooth curves, simple closed curves, and their orientation. Understand how curve direction affects integral values.

Cauchy's Theorem

Prove that ∮_C f(z) dz = 0 for analytic f on simply connected domains. Understand this as the fundamental theorem of complex analysis.

Cauchy's Integral Formula

Learn f(z₀) = (1/2πi) ∮_C f(z)/(z-z₀) dz. Understand how this expresses analytic functions in terms of their boundary values.

Derivatives from Integration

Prove f^(n)(z₀) = (n!/2πi) ∮_C f(z)/(z-z₀)^(n+1) dz. Understand how this shows infinite differentiability of analytic functions.

Maximum Modulus Principle

Prove that |f| attains its maximum on the boundary of domains for non-constant analytic functions. Learn applications to uniqueness theorems.

Liouville's Theorem

Prove that bounded entire functions are constant. Understand this as a fundamental rigidity result in complex analysis.

Fundamental Theorem of Algebra

Use Liouville's theorem to prove that every non-constant polynomial has a root. Understand this elegant complex-analytic proof.

Unit 5: Series and Sequences

Study convergence of complex series and power series expansions of analytic functions.

Complex Sequences and Series

Extend convergence concepts to ℂ. Learn that convergence means |z_n - L| → 0, and study absolute vs. conditional convergence.

Power Series

Study series of the form Σa_n(z-z₀)^n. Learn about convergence within circles and the radius of convergence formula.

Radius of Convergence

Apply the Cauchy-Hadamard theorem: R = 1/limsup|a_n|^(1/n). Learn ratio and root tests for determining convergence radius.

Taylor Series

Expand analytic functions as f(z) = Σf^(n)(z₀)(z-z₀)^n/n!. Understand that analytic functions are "locally polynomial."

Laurent Series

Extend to series with negative powers: f(z) = Σa_n(z-z₀)^n for all integers n. Learn expansion in annular regions.

Analytic Continuation

Extend analytic functions beyond their original domains using power series. Understand uniqueness and the identity theorem.

Uniform Convergence

Study when series converge uniformly on compact sets. Learn Weierstrass M-test and how uniform convergence preserves analyticity.

Weierstrass Theorems

Learn that uniform limits of analytic functions are analytic, and that series can be differentiated and integrated term by term.

Unit 6: Residue Theory

Master the residue theorem and its applications to evaluating complex and real integrals.

Isolated Singularities

Classify singularities using Laurent series: removable (no negative powers), poles (finite negative powers), essential (infinite negative powers).

Residues and Residue Theorem

Define Res(f,z₀) as the coefficient of (z-z₀)^(-1) in Laurent expansion. Learn ∮f(z)dz = 2πi Σ residues inside contour.

Classification of Singularities

Distinguish removable singularities, poles of various orders, and essential singularities. Learn tests using limits and Laurent series.

Computing Residues

Learn formulas for residues at simple poles: Res(f,z₀) = lim(z-z₀)f(z), and higher-order poles using derivatives.

Real Integral Evaluation

Use residue theorem to evaluate ∫₋∞^∞ f(x)dx by integrating over semicircular contours and applying residue theorem.

Trigonometric Integrals

Evaluate ∫₀^(2π) R(cos θ, sin θ)dθ by substituting z = e^(iθ) and integrating around the unit circle.

Improper Integrals

Handle integrals with branch cuts and multivalued functions. Learn keyhole contours and other specialized techniques.

Jordan's Lemma

Learn conditions for ∫_(semicircle) f(z)dz → 0 as radius → ∞. Essential for applying residue theorem to real integrals.

Unit 7: Conformal Mappings

Study angle-preserving transformations and their applications to physics and engineering.

Conformal Mappings

Learn that analytic functions with f'(z) ≠ 0 preserve angles and shapes locally. Understand the geometric significance of conformality.

Linear Fractional Transformations

Study f(z) = (az+b)/(cz+d) as the most general conformal maps of the extended plane. Learn about fixed points and classification.

Cross-Ratios

Learn the invariant (z,w;u,v) = ((z-u)(w-v))/((z-v)(w-u)) preserved by linear fractional transformations. Apply to mapping problems.

Riemann Mapping Theorem

Understand that any simply connected domain (except ℂ) can be mapped conformally to the unit disk. Learn about the significance and limitations.

Schwarz-Christoffel Formula

Learn the explicit formula for mapping the upper half-plane to polygonal regions. Understand parameter determination and applications.

Potential Theory Applications

Apply conformal mappings to solve Laplace's equation in complex domains. Understand how conformality preserves harmonic functions.

Fluid Flow Applications

Model 2D incompressible flow using complex velocity potentials. Learn how conformal mappings transform flow patterns around obstacles.

Electrostatic Applications

Solve electrostatic problems using conformal mappings. Understand how electric field lines and equipotentials transform under conformal maps.

Unit 8: Special Functions

Explore important special functions that arise naturally in complex analysis.

Gamma Function

Study Γ(z) = ∫₀^∞ t^(z-1)e^(-t) dt for Re(z) > 0 and its analytic continuation. Learn functional equation Γ(z+1) = zΓ(z).

Beta Function

Learn B(z,w) = ∫₀¹ t^(z-1)(1-t)^(w-1) dt and its relation to gamma function: B(z,w) = Γ(z)Γ(w)/Γ(z+w).

Riemann Zeta Function

Study ζ(s) = Σn^(-s) for Re(s) > 1 and its analytic continuation. Learn about zeros, poles, and connection to prime numbers.

Elliptic Functions

Study doubly periodic functions on ℂ. Learn about fundamental domains, Weierstrass ℘-function, and modular transformations.

Theta Functions

Learn Jacobi theta functions and their role in elliptic function theory. Understand transformation properties and applications.

Bessel Functions

Study solutions to Bessel's differential equation. Learn series representations, asymptotic behavior, and applications to physics.

Hypergeometric Functions

Study ₂F₁(a,b;c;z) and its properties. Learn how many special functions are special cases of hypergeometric functions.

Asymptotic Expansions

Learn to find asymptotic series for special functions. Understand the difference between convergent and asymptotic series.

Unit 9: Topics in Advanced Complex Analysis

Explore advanced theorems and techniques in complex function theory.

Argument Principle

Learn (1/2πi)∮(f'/f)dz = N - P where N = zeros, P = poles inside contour (counting multiplicity). Apply to root counting.

Rouché's Theorem

If |g(z)| < |f(z)| on |z| = r, then f and f+g have the same number of zeros inside |z| = r. Learn applications to polynomial roots.

Maximum Modulus Principle

Deepen understanding of maximum modulus principle and its consequences: minimum modulus principle, open mapping theorem, and uniqueness results.

Schwarz Lemma

If f: D → D with f(0) = 0, then |f(z)| ≤ |z| and |f'(0)| ≤ 1. Learn the rigidity: equality characterizes automorphisms.

Phragmén-Lindelöf Principle

Extend maximum principle to unbounded domains using growth conditions. Learn applications to uniqueness problems in strips and sectors.

Entire Functions

Study functions analytic on all of ℂ. Learn Liouville's theorem, Hadamard's factorization theorem, and order of growth classification.

Meromorphic Functions

Study functions with only poles as singularities. Learn partial fraction decompositions and the connection to rational functions.

Normal Families

Study families of analytic functions with uniform bounds. Learn Montel's theorem and applications to iteration theory and dynamics.

Unit 10: Applications and Connections

Connect complex analysis to other areas of mathematics, physics, and engineering.

Fourier and Laplace Transforms

Use complex analysis to understand transforms F(ω) = ∫f(t)e^(-iωt)dt. Learn about poles, residues, and inversion formulas.

Quantum Mechanics

Apply complex analysis to quantum mechanics: wave functions, scattering theory, and the role of analytic continuation in physics.

Number Theory Connections

Learn how complex analysis proves theorems in number theory: prime number theorem, Dirichlet's theorem on primes in arithmetic progressions.

Algebraic Geometry

Understand connections to Riemann surfaces, algebraic curves, and how complex analysis provides geometric intuition for abstract algebra.

Differential Equations

Apply complex methods to ODEs: Laplace transforms, series solutions, and asymptotic analysis of solutions in the complex plane.

Mathematical Physics

Study applications to electromagnetism, fluid dynamics, heat conduction, and wave propagation using complex variable techniques.

Signal Processing

Apply complex analysis to digital signal processing: z-transforms, filter design, and stability analysis using pole-zero plots.

Control Theory

Use complex analysis in control systems: transfer functions, stability criteria using Nyquist plots, and root-locus techniques.
