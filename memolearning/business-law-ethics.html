Sources of Law

Learn the various sources from which laws originate and their hierarchy in the legal system.

Constitutional Law Statutory Law Case Law
Laws governing business come from multiple sources arranged in a hierarchy: Constitutional law (highest), statutory law (federal and state), administrative regulations, and case law (judicial decisions). Understanding this hierarchy is crucial for legal compliance.

Court System Structure

Understand how federal and state court systems are organized and their jurisdictions.

Dual Court System:
• Federal Courts: Handle federal law, interstate commerce, constitutional issues
• State Courts: Handle state law, local matters, most business disputes
• Hierarchy: Trial courts → Appellate courts → Supreme courts
Jurisdiction Example: A contract dispute between companies in different states may be heard in federal court under diversity jurisdiction, while a local business licensing issue would be handled in state court.

Common Law vs Civil Law

Understand the differences between common law and civil law systems and their business implications.

Common law systems (like the US and UK) rely heavily on judicial precedent and case law, while civil law systems (like most of Europe) rely primarily on written codes and statutes. This affects how business law develops and is interpreted.
International Business Alert: When doing business internationally, understanding whether a country uses common law or civil law is crucial for contract drafting, dispute resolution, and legal strategy.
