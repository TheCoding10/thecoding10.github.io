Unit 1: Introduction to Ecology

Explore the fundamental concepts and scope of ecological science.

What is Ecology

Understand the scientific study of interactions between organisms and their environment.

Interactions Environment Systems
Ecology is the scientific study of the relationships between living organisms and their physical environment. It encompasses both biotic (living) and abiotic (non-living) components.
Key areas: population dynamics, community structure, ecosystem function, evolution

Biotic and Abiotic Factors

Distinguish between living and non-living environmental components.

Biotic Abiotic
Biotic factors include all living organisms (plants, animals, microbes), while abiotic factors include physical and chemical components (temperature, water, soil, nutrients).
