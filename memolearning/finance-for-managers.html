Time Value of Money

Master the fundamental concept that money available today is worth more than the same amount in the future.

The time value of money is based on the principle that money can earn returns over time. A dollar today is worth more than a dollar tomorrow due to its earning potential, inflation, and risk factors.
Future Value = Present Value √ó (1 + interest rate)^number of periods
FV = PV √ó (1 + r)^n
Example: If you invest $1,000 today at 8% annual interest, it will be worth $1,080 after one year. Conversely, $1,080 received one year from now has a present value of $1,000 at 8% discount rate.
