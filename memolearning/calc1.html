Unit 1: Limits and Continuity

The foundation of calculus: understanding how functions behave as inputs approach specific values.

Limit Definition

Understand the formal and intuitive definitions of limits, including notation and conceptual meaning.

Evaluating Limits

Learn algebraic techniques like factoring, rationalizing, and direct substitution to find limits.

One-sided Limits

Explore left-hand and right-hand limits, and understand when two-sided limits exist.

Infinite Limits

Analyze behavior near vertical asymptotes and understand when limits approach infinity.

Continuity

Define continuity at a point and on intervals, identifying different types of discontinuities.

Intermediate Value Theorem

Apply IVT to guarantee the existence of solutions and understand its geometric interpretation.

Limits at Infinity

Determine horizontal asymptotes and end behavior of rational and other functions.

Squeeze Theorem

Use the squeeze theorem to evaluate limits of oscillating functions and complex expressions.

Unit 2: Derivatives

The derivative as the fundamental tool for measuring rates of change and slopes of curves.

Definition of Derivative

Understand the derivative as the limit of difference quotients and its geometric meaning.

Tangent Lines

Find equations of tangent lines using the derivative and understand the geometric interpretation.

Differentiability

Explore the relationship between differentiability and continuity, identifying non-differentiable points.

Basic Rules

Master the power rule, constant rule, sum rule, and difference rule for differentiation.

Polynomial Derivatives

Apply differentiation rules to find derivatives of polynomial functions of any degree.

Higher-Order Derivatives

Calculate second, third, and higher derivatives, understanding their physical interpretations.

Unit 3: Differentiation Rules

Advanced techniques for finding derivatives of complex and composite functions.

Product Rule

Differentiate products of functions using (uv)' = u'v + uv' and apply to complex problems.

Quotient Rule

Find derivatives of quotients using (u/v)' = (u'v - uv')/v¬≤ and simplify results.

Chain Rule

Master the chain rule for composite functions and apply to nested function compositions.

Trigonometric Derivatives

Learn derivatives of sin, cos, tan, sec, csc, and cot functions with applications.

Exponential Derivatives

Differentiate exponential functions including e^x, a^x, and exponential compositions.

Logarithmic Derivatives

Find derivatives of ln(x), log base a, and logarithmic functions with chain rule.

Inverse Trig Derivatives

Differentiate arcsin, arccos, arctan and other inverse trigonometric functions.

Implicit Differentiation

Find derivatives of implicitly defined functions and solve related rate problems.

Unit 4: Applications of Derivatives

Use derivatives to analyze function behavior, solve optimization problems, and model real-world scenarios.

Critical Points

Find critical points where f'(x) = 0 or undefined, and classify local maxima and minima.

First Derivative Test

Use the sign of f'(x) to determine increasing/decreasing intervals and local extrema.

Second Derivative Test

Apply f''(x) to classify critical points and determine concavity of functions.

Concavity & Inflection

Analyze concave up/down regions and find inflection points using the second derivative.

Curve Sketching

Combine all derivative information to sketch accurate graphs of complex functions.

Optimization

Solve real-world maximum and minimum problems using calculus techniques.

Mean Value Theorem

Understand and apply MVT to guarantee the existence of specific derivative values.

L'H√¥pital's Rule

Evaluate indeterminate forms using L'H√¥pital's rule for limits involving derivatives.

Unit 5: Integrals

Introduction to antiderivatives and the Fundamental Theorem connecting derivatives and integrals.

Antiderivatives

Understand antiderivatives as reverse derivatives and find indefinite integrals.

Basic Integration Rules

Learn power rule for integration, constant rule, and sum/difference rules.

Riemann Sums

Approximate area under curves using left, right, midpoint, and trapezoidal sums.

Definite Integrals

Understand definite integrals as limits of Riemann sums and their geometric meaning.

Fundamental Theorem

Master both parts of FTC connecting differentiation and integration processes.

Properties of Integrals

Learn linearity, additivity, and comparison properties of definite integrals.

Net Change Theorem

Apply integration to find total change from rate of change functions.

Average Value

Calculate the average value of functions over intervals using integration.
