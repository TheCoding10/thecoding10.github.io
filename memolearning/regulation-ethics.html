Unit 1: Financial Regulation Fundamentals

Understand the foundations and purpose of financial regulation in modern markets.

Purpose and Goals of Financial Regulation

Learn why financial markets require regulation and the primary objectives of regulatory frameworks.

Regulatory Theories and Approaches

Explore different theoretical approaches to regulation including public interest and capture theories.

Market Failures and Regulation

Understand how market failures justify regulatory intervention in financial markets.

Regulatory Capture and Challenges

Examine the challenges of regulatory capture and how it affects regulatory effectiveness.

Cost-Benefit Analysis of Regulation

Learn to evaluate the economic impacts and trade-offs of regulatory interventions.

Regulatory Impact Assessment

Master the process of assessing and measuring the effectiveness of regulatory measures.

International Regulatory Coordination

Understand how regulators coordinate across borders to address global financial risks.

Evolution of Financial Regulation

Trace the historical development of financial regulation from early banking laws to modern frameworks.

Unit 2: Securities Regulation

Master the regulatory framework governing securities markets and offerings.

Securities Act of 1933

Learn the foundational law governing securities offerings and registration requirements.

Securities Exchange Act of 1934

Understand the comprehensive framework for securities trading and market regulation.

Investment Company Act of 1940

Master the regulations governing mutual funds and other investment companies.

Investment Advisers Act of 1940

Learn the regulatory requirements for investment advisers and their fiduciary duties.

Sarbanes-Oxley Act

Understand the corporate governance and financial reporting reforms following Enron and WorldCom.

Dodd-Frank Act Provisions

Explore the comprehensive financial reform legislation and its securities market implications.

JOBS Act and Crowdfunding

Learn about capital formation reforms and new funding mechanisms for emerging companies.

SEC Enforcement and Penalties

Understand SEC enforcement powers, penalty structures, and recent enforcement trends.

Unit 3: Banking Regulation

Learn the comprehensive regulatory framework for banking institutions.

Bank Holding Company Act

Understand the regulation of bank holding companies and their non-banking activities.

Glass-Steagall Act (Historical)

Learn about the historical separation of commercial and investment banking and its repeal.

Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act

Explore the financial modernization that allowed universal banking and privacy protections.

Basel Capital Requirements

Master international capital adequacy standards and risk-weighted asset calculations.

Dodd-Frank Banking Provisions

Understand comprehensive banking reforms including systemically important bank designation.

Volcker Rule Implementation

Learn about proprietary trading restrictions and their impact on banking operations.

Community Reinvestment Act

Understand requirements for banks to serve the credit needs of their entire communities.

Bank Examination and Supervision

Learn about the bank examination process and supervisory rating systems like CAMELS.
