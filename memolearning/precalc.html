Unit 1: Composite and Inverse Functions

Core ideas: what functions are, composing/decomposing them, and finding/using inverses.

Function Composition

Learn to evaluate (f‚àòg)(x), understand composition order, and decompose complex functions into simpler parts.

Domains and Ranges

Understand how composition affects domains and ranges, including restrictions from inner functions.

One-to-One Functions

Use the horizontal line test to determine if functions are one-to-one and eligible for inverses.

Inverse Functions

Find, verify, and work with inverse functions using proper notation and algebraic techniques.

Domain Restrictions

Learn when and how to restrict domains to create inverse functions for non-one-to-one functions.

Solving with Inverses

Apply inverse functions to solve equations and understand the relationship between functions and their inverses.

Unit 2: Trigonometry

Deep dive into trig functions, identities, equations, and their graphs.

Angle Measure & Arc Length

Convert between degrees and radians, calculate arc length and sector area.

Unit Circle

Master unit circle values, reference angles, and coordinate relationships.

Trig Function Graphs

Understand amplitude, period, phase shift, and vertical shift in sine, cosine, and tangent graphs.

Trig Identities

Learn Pythagorean, reciprocal, quotient, co-function, and even/odd identities.

Sum & Difference Identities

Apply sum, difference, double-angle, and half-angle identities to simplify expressions.

Inverse Trig Functions

Understand domains, ranges, and applications of inverse trigonometric functions.

Trig Equations

Solve trigonometric equations using algebraic and graphical methods.

Law of Sines & Cosines

Apply laws of sines and cosines to solve triangles and find areas.
