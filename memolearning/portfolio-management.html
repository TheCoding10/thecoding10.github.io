Unit 1: Portfolio Theory Foundations

Master the fundamental principles of modern portfolio theory and investment science.

Modern Portfolio Theory

Understand Harry Markowitz's groundbreaking theory for optimal portfolio construction based on risk and return trade-offs.

Risk and Return Concepts

Learn to measure and analyze the fundamental relationship between investment risk and expected returns.

Efficient Frontier Theory

Construct efficient portfolios that maximize return for a given level of risk or minimize risk for a given return.

Capital Allocation Line

Understand how to combine risky portfolios with risk-free assets to optimize investor utility.

Two-Fund Theorem

Learn how all optimal portfolios can be constructed using just two funds: a risk-free asset and the market portfolio.

Separation Theorem

Understand how investment decisions can be separated into portfolio selection and capital allocation decisions.

Utility Theory Applications

Apply utility theory to understand investor preferences and optimal portfolio choices based on risk tolerance.

Mean-Variance Optimization

Master the mathematical framework for constructing portfolios that optimize the risk-return trade-off.

Unit 2: Asset Allocation Strategies

Learn strategic and tactical asset allocation approaches for optimal portfolio construction.

Strategic Asset Allocation

Develop long-term asset allocation strategies based on investor goals, risk tolerance, and time horizon.

Tactical Asset Allocation

Learn to make short-term adjustments to asset allocation based on market conditions and opportunities.

Dynamic Asset Allocation

Implement systematic approaches that automatically adjust asset allocation based on market conditions.

Age-Based Allocation Models

Apply age-appropriate asset allocation rules and lifecycle investment strategies for different life stages.

Target-Date Strategies

Understand how target-date funds automatically adjust asset allocation as investors approach retirement.

Global Asset Allocation

Incorporate international assets and currencies into portfolio allocation for enhanced diversification.

Alternative Asset Integration

Learn to include alternative investments like real estate, commodities, and private equity in asset allocation.

Lifecycle Investing

Adapt investment strategies to different life stages and changing financial needs over time.

Unit 3: Risk Management

Identify, measure, and manage various types of portfolio risk effectively.

Risk Measurement Techniques

Master various methods for quantifying portfolio risk including standard deviation, beta, and tracking error.

Value at Risk (VaR)

Calculate and interpret Value at Risk measures to estimate potential portfolio losses over specific time periods.

Expected Shortfall

Understand conditional VaR and expected shortfall measures for tail risk assessment and management.

Stress Testing Methods
