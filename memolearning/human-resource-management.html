What is Business Analytics

Learn the fundamental definition and scope of business analytics in organizational decision-making.

Data-Driven Decision Making Insights
Business Analytics is the practice of iterative, methodical exploration of an organization's data, with emphasis on statistical analysis, to drive business planning and decision-making. It transforms raw data into meaningful insights for strategic advantage.

Types of Analytics

Understand the four main types of analytics and their applications in business.

Four Types of Analytics:
‚Ä¢ Descriptive: What happened? (Reporting, dashboards)
‚Ä¢ Diagnostic: Why did it happen? (Root cause analysis)
‚Ä¢ Predictive: What will happen? (Forecasting, modeling)
‚Ä¢ Prescriptive: What should we do? (Optimization, recommendations)
Analytics Progression: Organizations typically mature through these analytics types sequentially, with each level building on the previous one. Most companies start with descriptive analytics before advancing to predictive and prescriptive approaches.

Learn how to structure decision-making processes around data and analytics.

Data-driven decision making (DDDM) is a process that involves collecting data based on measurable goals or KPIs, analyzing patterns and facts from these insights, and utilizing them to develop strategies and activities that benefit the business.
