Unit 2: Prospect Theory

Master Kahneman and Tversky's groundbreaking theory of decision-making under uncertainty.

Expected Utility Theory Limitations

Understand the shortcomings of traditional expected utility theory in explaining real human behavior.

Value Function Characteristics

Learn about the S-shaped value function that is concave for gains and convex for losses.

Reference Point Dependence

Explore how outcomes are evaluated relative to a reference point rather than absolute wealth levels.

Loss Aversion Principles

Understand why losses feel roughly twice as painful as equivalent gains feel pleasurable.

Probability Weighting

Learn how people systematically distort probabilities when making decisions under uncertainty.

Certainty Effect

Discover why people overweight outcomes that are considered certain compared to probable outcomes.

Reflection Effect

Understand how risk preferences reverse when moving from gains to losses domain.

Financial Applications

Apply prospect theory to explain various financial behaviors and market phenomena.

Unit 3: Cognitive Biases in Finance

Identify and understand systematic errors in thinking that affect financial decisions.

Overconfidence Bias

Learn how excessive confidence in one's abilities leads to poor investment decisions and excessive trading.

Confirmation Bias

Understand the tendency to seek information that confirms existing beliefs while ignoring contradictory evidence.

Anchoring and Adjustment

Explore how initial information serves as an anchor that influences subsequent judgments and decisions.

Availability Heuristic

Learn how easily recalled information is overweighted in probability assessments and decision-making.

Representativeness Bias

Understand how stereotyping and pattern recognition can lead to systematic errors in financial judgments.
