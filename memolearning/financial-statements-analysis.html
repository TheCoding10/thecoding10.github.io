Unit 1: Financial Statements Overview

Understand the purpose, structure, and components of the primary financial statements.

Purpose of Financial Statements

Learn why companies prepare financial statements and how they communicate business performance to stakeholders.

Users of Financial Information

Identify different stakeholders who rely on financial statements including investors, creditors, and management.

GAAP vs IFRS Standards

Compare Generally Accepted Accounting Principles with International Financial Reporting Standards and their impact on analysis.

Financial Reporting Framework

Understand the conceptual framework that guides financial statement preparation and presentation.

Annual Reports and 10-K Filings

Navigate annual reports and SEC filings to extract key financial and business information for analysis.

Quarterly Reports (10-Q)

Analyze interim financial statements and understand their limitations and seasonal considerations.

Management Discussion & Analysis

Extract valuable insights from MD&A sections about company strategy, risks, and future outlook.

Auditor's Report Significance

Understand different types of audit opinions and their implications for financial statement reliability.

Unit 2: Income Statement Analysis

Master the analysis of revenues, expenses, and profitability measures.

Revenue Recognition Principles

Understand when and how companies recognize revenue and identify potential manipulation red flags.

Cost of Goods Sold Analysis

Analyze COGS components, inventory methods, and their impact on profitability and cash flow.

Gross Profit Margins

Calculate and interpret gross profit margins to assess pricing power and operational efficiency.

Operating Expense Evaluation

Break down operating expenses to identify cost structure, efficiency trends, and management effectiveness.

EBITDA Calculations

Calculate earnings before interest, taxes, depreciation, and amortization and understand its uses and limitations.

Non-operating Items

Identify and adjust for non-recurring and non-operating items to assess core business performance.

Earnings Per Share Metrics

Calculate basic and diluted EPS and understand their significance for equity valuation.

Quality of Earnings Assessment

Evaluate the sustainability and cash backing of reported earnings through quality analysis techniques.

Unit 3: Balance Sheet Analysis

Evaluate assets, liabilities, and equity to assess financial position and strength.

Asset Classification and Quality

Assess different asset categories and evaluate their quality, liquidity, and potential for value creation.

Current vs Non-current Assets

Distinguish between short-term and long-term assets and their implications for liquidity analysis.

Working Capital Analysis

Calculate and interpret working capital components to assess short-term financial health and efficiency.

Fixed Asset Evaluation

Analyze property, plant, and equipment for age, utilization, and capital investment requirements.

Intangible Assets Assessment

Evaluate goodwill, patents, and other intangibles for value and impairment risks.

Liability Structure Analysis

Examine current and long-term liabilities to assess financial obligations and payment schedules.

Debt Composition Evaluation

Analyze debt maturity profiles, interest rates, and covenant restrictions for risk assessment.

Shareholders' Equity Components

Break down equity components including retained earnings
