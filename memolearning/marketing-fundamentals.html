Marketing vs Selling

Understand the fundamental differences between marketing and selling approaches.

Selling Concept: Inside-out perspective, focuses on existing products, short-term sales goals
Marketing Concept: Outside-in perspective, focuses on customer needs, long-term relationships
Key Difference: Selling starts with products, Marketing starts with customers
Customer Insight: "People don't want to buy a quarter-inch drill. They want a quarter-inch hole." - Theodore Levitt. This illustrates the marketing concept of focusing on customer needs rather than product features.

Production Orientation

Learn about the production concept and when it's still relevant today.

Production orientation assumes customers prefer products that are widely available and affordable. Companies focus on improving production and distribution efficiency. Still relevant for commodity products and developing markets.
Historical Example: Henry Ford's Model T - "Any customer can have a car painted any color as long as it's black." Ford focused on production efficiency and cost reduction rather than customer preferences.
