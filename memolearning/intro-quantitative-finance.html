Unit 1: Mathematical Foundations

Build essential mathematical skills needed for quantitative finance modeling.

Calculus Fundamentals

Master differential and integral calculus concepts essential for continuous-time financial modeling.

Linear Algebra Basics

Learn matrix operations, eigenvalues, and linear transformations for portfolio optimization and factor models.

Differential Equations

Solve ordinary and partial differential equations that arise in options pricing and interest rate modeling.

Optimization Techniques

Apply constrained and unconstrained optimization methods for portfolio selection and parameter estimation.

Matrix Operations

Perform advanced matrix calculations including decompositions and inverse operations for quantitative analysis.

Vector Calculus

Use multivariable calculus concepts for risk management and multi-dimensional optimization problems.

Fourier Transforms

Apply frequency domain analysis techniques for options pricing and signal processing in financial data.

Numerical Methods

Implement computational algorithms for solving mathematical problems that lack analytical solutions.

Unit 2: Probability & Statistics

Master probability theory and statistical methods for financial modeling.

Probability Distributions

Understand normal, lognormal, exponential, and other distributions commonly used in finance.

Random Variables

Work with discrete and continuous random variables and their properties in financial contexts.

Central Limit Theorem

Apply the central limit theorem to understand portfolio behavior and risk aggregation.

Hypothesis Testing

Perform statistical tests to validate financial models and investment strategies.

Regression Analysis

Build linear and nonlinear regression models to explain asset returns and risk factors.

Time Series Analysis

Model and forecast financial time series data using ARIMA, GARCH, and other methods.

Bayesian Statistics

Apply Bayesian methods for parameter estimation and model selection in finance.

Monte Carlo Methods

Use simulation techniques to solve complex financial problems and estimate risk measures.

Understand random processes and their applications in financial modeling.

Brownian Motion
