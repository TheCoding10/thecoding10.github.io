Understand the basic principles of investing and why it's essential for building wealth.

What is Investing vs Saving

Distinguish between saving money in low-risk accounts and investing for long-term growth and wealth building.

Power of Compound Interest

Understand how compound interest works over time to dramatically multiply your investment returns.

Time Value of Money

Learn why money today is worth more than money in the future and how this principle drives investment decisions.

Risk vs Return Relationship

Understand the fundamental principle that higher potential returns generally come with higher investment risks.

Investment Timeline Planning

Match your investment strategy to your time horizon, with different approaches for short, medium, and long-term goals.

Investment vs Speculation

Distinguish between prudent long-term investing and short-term speculation or gambling with money.

Dollar-Cost Averaging Basics

Learn the strategy of investing fixed amounts regularly to reduce the impact of market volatility on purchases.

Getting Started with Small Amounts

Discover how to begin investing even with limited funds through low-minimum accounts and fractional shares.

Unit 2: Investment Risk Management

Learn to assess, manage, and balance investment risks for optimal portfolio performance.

Types of Investment Risk

Identify various risks including market risk, inflation risk, interest rate risk, and company-specific risks.

Risk Tolerance Assessment

Evaluate your personal comfort level with investment volatility and potential losses to guide investment choices.

Risk vs Reward Analysis

Analyze potential investment returns relative to the risks involved to make informed investment decisions.

Diversification Principles

Reduce portfolio risk by spreading investments across different asset classes, sectors, and geographic regions.

Asset Allocation Strategies

Determine the optimal mix of stocks, bonds, and other assets based on your goals and risk tolerance.

Portfolio Rebalancing

Maintain your target asset allocation by periodically adjusting portfolio weights back to desired levels.

Market Volatility Management

Develop strategies to stay calm and make rational decisions during periods of significant market fluctuations.

Emergency Fund Before Investing

Ensure financial stability by building an emergency fund before committing money to long-term investments.
