Competitive Priorities

Understand the key competitive dimensions that operations can excel in to support business strategy.

Four Main Competitive Priorities:
• Cost: Competing on low prices through operational efficiency
• Quality: Competing on superior performance and reliability
• Time: Competing on speed of delivery and responsiveness
• Flexibility: Competing on customization and variety
Trade-off Reality: While companies strive to excel in all areas, resources are limited. Operations strategy involves making deliberate choices about which competitive priorities to emphasize while maintaining acceptable performance in others.

Order Qualifiers vs Winners

Distinguish between basic requirements and factors that actually win customers.

Order qualifiers are the minimum standards required to be considered by customers, while order winners are the factors that actually persuade customers to choose your product or service over competitors.
Evolution Over Time: Order winners often become order qualifiers as competitors catch up. What once differentiated a company (like quality in automobiles) may become the expected minimum standard, requiring new sources of competitive advantage.
