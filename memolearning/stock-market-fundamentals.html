Market Participants Overview

Identify different types of market participants including individual investors, institutions, market makers, and regulators.

Understand the relationship between stock market performance and broader economic conditions and indicators.

Learn the fundamentals of stocks as ownership shares in companies.

Stock Ownership Basics

Understand what it means to own stock in a company and the rights and responsibilities that come with ownership.

Common vs Preferred Stock

Compare the characteristics, rights, and benefits of common stock versus preferred stock investments.

Stock Certificates and Shares

Learn about physical and electronic stock certificates and how shares represent fractional ownership of companies.

Voting Rights and Dividends

Explore shareholder voting rights in corporate decisions and how dividends provide income to stockholders.

Stock Splits and Mergers

Understand corporate actions like stock splits that increase share count and mergers that combine companies.

IPOs and New Issues

Learn about Initial Public Offerings and how private companies go public by issuing stock to investors.

Stock Buybacks

Understand how companies repurchase their own shares and the impact on remaining shareholders and stock price.

Delisting and Bankruptcy

Learn what happens when companies are removed from stock exchanges or face bankruptcy and liquidation.

Explore how stock exchanges operate and facilitate securities trading.

NYSE Operations

Learn how the New York Stock Exchange operates with its hybrid floor and electronic trading systems.

NASDAQ Electronic Trading

Market Makers and Specialists

Explore the role of market makers and specialists in providing liquidity and facilitating smooth trading.

Order Matching Systems

Trading Hours and Sessions

Circuit Breakers and Halts

Alternative Trading Systems
