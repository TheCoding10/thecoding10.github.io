Unit 1: Economic Foundations

Understand fundamental economic principles essential for financial decision-making.

Scarcity and Opportunity Cost

Learn how limited resources force choices and understand opportunity cost as the value of the next best alternative.

Supply and Demand Basics

Master the fundamental forces that determine prices in markets and how they interact to create equilibrium.

Market Equilibrium Concepts

Understand how markets clear and prices adjust to balance supply and demand in competitive markets.

Economic Efficiency Principles

Explore allocative and productive efficiency and how markets can achieve optimal resource allocation.

Marginal Analysis Applications

Learn to make decisions by comparing marginal benefits and marginal costs in economic and financial contexts.

Economic Systems Overview

Compare different economic systems and understand how they affect resource allocation and financial markets.

Role of Prices in Markets

Understand how prices convey information, coordinate economic activity, and provide incentives for efficient allocation.

Economic Decision-Making Models

Apply rational choice theory and other models to understand how individuals and firms make economic decisions.

Unit 2: Microeconomic Theory

Master individual market behavior and its applications to financial markets.

Consumer Choice Theory

Learn how consumers make decisions under budget constraints and how preferences shape demand patterns.

Utility Maximization

Understand how rational consumers allocate their income to maximize satisfaction subject to budget constraints.

Demand Curve Derivation

Learn how individual demand curves are derived from utility maximization and how they aggregate to market demand.

Producer Theory Basics

Understand how firms make production decisions to maximize profits given technology and input costs.

Cost Structure Analysis

Analyze different types of costs (fixed, variable, marginal) and their impact on firm decision-making.

Market Structures Overview

Compare perfect competition, monopoly, oligopoly, and monopolistic competition and their implications for pricing.

Price Elasticity Concepts

Master price elasticity of demand and supply and their applications to revenue and policy analysis.

Game Theory Applications

Apply game theory concepts to understand strategic interactions in markets and financial decision-making.

Unit 3: Macroeconomic Fundamentals

Explore economy-wide phenomena that impact financial markets and investment decisions.

GDP Measurement and Components

Learn how Gross Domestic Product is measured and understand its components: consumption, investment, government, and net exports.

Economic Growth Factors

Identify the key drivers of long-term economic growth including capital, labor, and technological progress.

Business Cycle Patterns

Understand the phases of business cycles and their impact on different sectors and investment opportunities.
