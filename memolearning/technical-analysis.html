Unit 1: Technical Analysis Fundamentals

Understand the core principles and philosophy behind technical analysis of financial markets.

History and Evolution

Learn the origins of technical analysis from Japanese rice trading to modern computerized systems.

Core Assumptions and Principles

Master the three fundamental assumptions: market action discounts everything, prices move in trends, and history repeats.

Technical vs Fundamental Analysis

Understand the differences between price-based and value-based analysis approaches to markets.

Market Efficiency Debate

Explore the efficient market hypothesis and its implications for technical analysis effectiveness.

Price Action Concepts

Learn how price movements reflect all available information and market participant psychology.

Time Frames and Market Cycles

Understand different time horizons from intraday to long-term and their analytical implications.

Technical Analysis Limitations

Recognize the constraints and potential pitfalls of relying solely on technical analysis.

Behavioral Finance Connections

Connect technical patterns with underlying human psychology and behavioral biases in trading.

Unit 2: Chart Types and Construction

Master different chart types and understand how to read and interpret price data.

Line Charts and Their Uses

Understand the simplest chart type using closing prices to show overall price direction and trends.

Bar Charts (OHLC)

Learn to read Open-High-Low-Close bar charts that display complete price information for each period.

Candlestick Charts

Master Japanese candlestick charts that provide visual price action patterns and market sentiment.

Point and Figure Charts

Understand price-only charts that filter out time and focus purely on significant price movements.

Renko Charts

Learn time-independent charts that only show price moves of a predetermined size.

Volume Charts

Explore charts based on trading volume rather than time periods for volume-based analysis.

Chart Scaling and Time Frames

Understand linear vs logarithmic scaling and choose appropriate time frames for analysis.

Data Quality and Adjustments

Learn about dividend adjustments, stock splits, and data cleaning for accurate analysis.

Unit 3: Trend Analysis

Learn to identify, analyze, and trade with market trends using various technical tools.

Trend Definition and Identification

Learn the formal definition of trends and how to identify them using price action analysis.

Uptrends, Downtrends, and Sideways Markets

Classify market movements into the three basic trend directions and their characteristics.

Trendlines and Channels

Draw and interpret trendlines and channel lines to define trend boundaries and potential reversals.

Moving Averages

Use simple, exponential, and weighted moving averages to identify and follow trends.

Trend Strength Indicators

Measure the strength and sustainability of trends using various technical indicators.

Trend Reversal Signals

Identify early warning signs of potential trend changes and reversal patterns.

Multi-Timeframe Trend Analysis

Analyze trends across multiple time frames to understand the bigger picture context.

Trend Following Strategies

Develop systematic approaches to trade in the direction of established trends.
